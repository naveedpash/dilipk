Drug Induced Liver Injury

The burden of liver diseases in Pakistan is a significant healthcare concern, amounting to large socioeconomic losses and debilitating standards of living. Drug induced liver injury (DILI) is an entity that contributes to cases of hepatotoxicity, and is under investigation in different parts of the world. There is a dearth of data that would allow us to create a national database.

As a low-middle income country, Pakistan harbors a different landscape of infectious diseases including hepatitis, tuberculosis etc, as well as a growing number of chronic health conditions including cancer, diabetes, hypertension, etc. This directly translates into a wide spectrum of pathologies that utilize a similarly diverse set of pharmacological interventions, many of which can be severely damaging to the liver.

The DILIpk initiative is the first step in an attempt to define the extent of this disease, formulate collaboration between public and private sector healthcare facilities and pharmaceutical industries, and together create a national database that would pave the way forward for a better and a standardized approach to management of our patients with DILI.

Dr. Shahab Abid

Dr. Abid is currently heading the Section of Gastroenterology and Hepatology at AKU. He has vast experience in working as consultant physician and gastroenterologist in both private and public sector hospitals. He is heavily involved in Medical Education, teaching and mentoring students, residents, and fellows. He is also the certified examiner and assessor of fellowship examination of CPSP for Medicine. He is Chair of Department of Medicine Research Committee, and a member of University Seed Money Grant Committee and Postgraduate Medical Education Committee (PGMEC). He served as Director of GI Residency Program at AKU for five years. His scholarly interests have led him to procure research grants for clinical trials, and to serve as reviewer for several international and national peer reviewed journals. With more than 90 publications to his name, Dr. Abid continues to collaborate with universities, pharmaceutical industries, other regional hospitals, and international institutes to further academic practice in Gastroenterology. Dr. Abid is the member of executive council of Asia Pacific Association of Study of Liver (APASL), and serves as the General Secretary of Pakistan Society of Gastroenterology (PSG). When he is not at the hospital, Dr. Abid loves spending time with family, travelling, and catching up with his ever growing reading list.
